# Habit Lifecycle — Pause, Retire, Graduate

Habits evolve. HabitPower gives every habit four possible states so your tracking system can grow with you.

---

## The Four States

### Active
The default. Habit appears in daily check-in, counts toward streak and XP, reminder fires on schedule.

### Paused
Use this for **temporary interruptions** — travel, illness, a busy sprint at work.

- The habit disappears from your daily list.
- Streak calculation is suspended for this habit. Missing it doesn't count as a miss.
- Reminders are silenced.
- History is preserved exactly as-is.
- Reactivate when you're ready and the streak picks up where it left off.

### Retired
Use this when you've **decided to stop** a habit permanently, but don't want to lose the history.

- Excluded from daily tracking.
- History preserved and visible in data exports.
- The habit no longer contributes to XP or streaks going forward.

### Graduated
Use this when the habit is **no longer a habit — it's just who you are**.

- The habit moves to the **Identity Wall** on your Dashboard.
- No longer tracked daily. You don't need to track breathing.
- History preserved.
- The Identity Wall is a collapsible card at the bottom of the Dashboard — a quiet record of what you've internalized over time, not a graveyard.

---

## How to Change a Habit's Lifecycle

1. Open **Admin › Habits**
2. Find the habit and look for the status dropdown in the card's action row
3. Select the new status — a description is shown for each option
4. The change takes effect immediately

---

## Why This Matters

Without lifecycle states, long-term users face a choice between two bad options: delete a habit (losing history) or leave dead habits cluttering the daily list (losing clarity). Productive people evolve their systems. The app should support that gracefully.

The Identity Wall in particular is designed to be a point of quiet pride — a visual reminder that your consistency compounded into something real.
